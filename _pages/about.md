I am an ecologist who specializes in developing and applying quantitative methods for solving problems in ecology and conservation biology. During my Ph.D. and PostDoc, I worked with alternative conservation mechanisms in cork oak woodlands, such as forest certification and payments for ecosystem services. More recently, I became interested in analyzing ecological patterns across different spatial scales. I have a solid background in both frequentist and Bayesian statistics and on geographic information systems. I also have considerable field experience, having spent a significant amount of time analyzing Mediterranean vegetation patterns, the ecological integrity of wadeable rivers, and the spatial ecology of mammals and birds. Outside academia, I have been working as a consultant on biodiversity and ecosystem services for WWF Portugal and WWF Mediterranean. Although most of my work keeps me indoors, I am the happiest outside.
